This time, you find yourself in the midst of a murder mystery! Somebody has killed the butler (just to ensure that he didn't do it). Your job is to identify who the murderer is, where the murder took place and what the weapon used was.

You know the following to be true:

  • There were only seven people in the grounds at the time; the butler and the six suspects below.
  • The murder took place at midday.
  • The murderer was alone with the butler when the murder took place; there was nobody else in the location with them.
  • Each suspect has made two statements to the police, which are below.
  • The police have determined that both of the murderer's statements are lies.
  • The police also know that two other people, neither of whom are the murderer, were having an affair. To cover it up, both people having an affair have told one truth and one lie. No other characters are having affairs.
  • Both statements made by the other three suspects are completely true.

You consider asking the police how they have such specific knowledge, but you are not able to as ultimately the police are mostly just a framing device for the puzzle.

Note that a statement is a lie if it is not completely true - e.g. one of the lying characters above could say as a lie "I have a dog and I have a cat" and still have a cat, provided they did not have a dog.

The possible weapons are:

  • Knife
  • Gun
  • Poison
  • Statuette

The possible rooms are:

  • Outhouse
  • Conservatory
  • Kitchen
  • Library

The cook

  • I was in the kitchen all day and nobody else came in.
  • The murderer was either the maid or the barber.

The gardener

  • The poison was unused and could not have been the murder weapon.
  • It was raining all day on the day of the murder.

The maid

  • The murder weapon was either the knife or the statuette.
  • The barber is having an affair.

The driver

  • The stable boy neither committed the murder nor was having an affair.
  • On the day of the murder I took the stable boy to the outhouse and we spent the whole day there; it was a lovely, sunny day.

The barber

  • I was in the conservatory all day with the statuette.
  • Between them, the cook, gardener and driver told exactly two lies.

The stable boy

  • The couple having the affair were both in the kitchen all day.
  • It was sunny all day on the day of the murder.

Submit your answer as a comma separated list of person, weapon and place. So if you think the murderer was the waiter with a pickaxe in the garden, then a) you've got a really left-field solution to the question; but more importantly b) you'd submit "waiter,pickaxe,garden" (with no spaces)

"Alas!" cries the maid. "You saw through my incredibly cunning plan through the power of logic. It was indeed me! But I did it for good reasons! I did it for this jigsaw piece!" She shows you the piece below. This is clearly very important to her, and so when she's not looking you steal it off her. That seems like more than a fair punishment for murder.
